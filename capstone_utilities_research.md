# 2. The Core Problem

Utilities are **legally required** to run energy efficiency programs via state mandates — but the federal funding they relied on is being cut or frozen. Programs stall mid-cycle. Customers and contractors are left in limbo. Utilities cannot solve this internally.

**APTIM's opportunity:** Help utilities keep programs alive when the money is unreliable.

---

# 3. Federal Funding Landscape (2024–2026)

## IRA HOMES & HEAR Programs
- **Total allocation:** $8.8 billion ($4.3B HOMES + $4.5B HEAR)
- **Frozen:** January 20, 2025 — Trump executive order "Unleashing American Energy"
- **Live programs as of Feb 2026:** Only 23 states have active programs
- Wisconsin was **first in the nation** to launch (HOMES Aug 2024, HEAR Dec 2024) — $149M allocated — then the federal freeze hit mid-delivery

## One Big Beautiful Bill (Signed July 4, 2025)
- **Sections 25C & 25D** (consumer energy tax credits) — **eliminated December 31, 2025**
- These were the primary tools driving consumer participation in utility programs
- HOMES and HEAR **survived** — now more critical than ever as the only federal tool remaining

## DOE Weatherization Assistance Program (WAP)
- FY2026: ~$329M — survived cuts but not growing
- Federal staffing gutted — administration problems even where dollar funding survived

---

# 4. State Mandates vs. Funding Reality

- **26 states + DC** have Energy Efficiency Resource Standards (EERS) — legal savings targets utilities must hit regardless of federal funding
- These states account for **80%+ of all national utility EE savings**
- Utilities historically hit ~99% of goals — but often via banked savings and carry-overs
- Without federal co-funding, the **cost per kWh saved goes up** for ratepayers
- **Low-income program carve-outs** are most exposed — relied most heavily on IRA HEAR and WAP
- **States without EERS** have no legal floor — programs can simply be cut when federal money disappears

# 6. Focus on Energy (Wisconsin) — Deep Dive

> This is APTIM's flagship utility program — the clearest example of the funding gap problem in action.

## Program Structure
| | |
|---|---|
| **Funder** | Wisconsin IOUs required by state law to contribute 1.2% of annual retail revenue |
| **Annual Budget** | ~$95 million |
| **Administrator** | APTIM Government Solutions, LLC (via SEERA contract) |
| **Scale** | 107 utility territories, 50,000+ projects/year, ~190,000 households in 2024 |
| **ROI** | $5 in benefits for every $1 invested |

## How It Works With Utilities
```
Utilities contribute 1.2% of retail revenue
        ↓
Funds go to SEERA (state entity)
        ↓
SEERA contracts APTIM to administer everything
        ↓
Utilities recover costs through approved rates
        ↓
IRA programs (HOMES/HEAR) sit ON TOP as separate portfolios
```

## The Funding Gap Dynamic
- Demand for Focus on Energy benefits is **outpacing the $95M budget**
- A proposed bill (LRB-3626) would double utility contributions to 2.4% — **not yet law**
- When IRA overlay funds freeze, customers mid-project + contractors who geared up are left in limbo
- APTIM must simultaneously: manage triage, renegotiate subcontractor scopes, maintain regulatory compliance

# 9. The Gap APTIM Can Fill

When federal funding freezes mid-program, utilities face 6 problems they **cannot solve internally:**

| Problem | Why Utilities Can't Handle It |
|---|---|
| Contractor network in limbo | Utilities have no field staff to maintain trade ally relationships |
| Customer pipeline management | Generic call centers can't manage program-specific claims and complaints |
| Budget re-architecture | Rapidly re-modeling program economics needs TPA-level expertise |
| Compliance risk reallocation | EERS targets must still be met — TPA can model alternative measures to scale up |
| Grant closeout documentation | EM&V audit trails required even mid-pause |
| Rapid program redesign | Utilities move slowly; TPA provides capacity without 12-month procurement cycle |

# 10. Emerging Service Concept (3 Options)

## Option A — Program Continuity Service ⭐ Strongest
APTIM offers a **standing "program continuity" contract** that utilities activate when federal/state funding freezes. Covers: contractor network maintenance, customer pipeline management, compliance documentation, and rapid program redesign.

*Why it works:* Directly solves the mid-program freeze problem. Utilities pay for insurance against disruption rather than scrambling after the fact.

## Option B — Alternative Funding Stacking
APTIM helps utilities identify and access funding beyond federal grants:
- Capacity market revenues (efficiency programs reduce peak demand — monetizable)
- Carbon credit markets (energy savings as carbon offsets)
- On-bill financing (customers repay through utility bills — no grant needed)
- Green bonds (ESG-rated bonds for efficiency programs)
- Utility CSR/foundation funds

## Option C — Small-Device Bridge Program
Low-cost small-device program (smart thermostats, LEDs, efficient appliances) keeps utilities engaged and customer pipelines warm during funding gaps. Compounds into larger upgrades (heat pumps, insulation) when funding returns.

*Why it works:* Lower cost-per-customer means programs stay alive on smaller budgets. Behavioral economics supports it — customers who take one small action are significantly more likely to take a larger one.
