## The Core Problem (Plain English)

Utilities are legally required to run energy efficiency programs but the federal funding they relied on is being cut or frozen. Programs stall mid-cycle, customers and contractors are left in limbo, and utilities cannot solve it internally. APTIM's opportunity is to help utilities keep programs alive when the money is unreliable.

---

## Federal Funding Landscape (2024–2026)

### IRA HOMES & HEAR Programs
- **Total allocation:** $8.8 billion ($4.3B HOMES + $4.5B HEAR)
- **Status:** Frozen January 20, 2025 via Trump executive order "Unleashing American Energy"
- **Live programs as of Feb 2026:** Only 23 states (AZ, CA, CO, CT, HI, IL, ME, MD, MA, MI, MN, NV, NH, NJ, NM, NY, OR, PA, RI, VT, VA, WA, WI)
- **Wisconsin** was first in the nation to launch HOMES (Aug 1, 2024) and HEAR Phase 1 (Dec 18, 2024) — $149M total allocated — then federal freeze hit mid-delivery

### One Big Beautiful Bill (Signed July 4, 2025)
- **Section 25C** (Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit) — eliminated effective December 31, 2025
- **Section 25D** (Residential Clean Energy Credit) — eliminated effective December 31, 2025
- These were the primary consumer-facing co-investment tools that drove participation in utility programs
- HOMES and HEAR programs survived the bill — now more important than ever

### DOE Weatherization Assistance Program (WAP)
- FY2026 appropriation: ~$329M — survived but not growing
- Federal staffing gutted, creating administration problems even where dollar funding survived

## State Mandates vs. Funding Reality

- **26 states + DC** have Energy Efficiency Resource Standards (EERS) — legal savings targets utilities must hit
- These states account for **80%+ of national utility EE program savings**
- Utilities historically achieved ~99% of EERS goals — but often via banked savings and carry-overs
- **Without federal co-funding**, the per-dollar cost of achieving the same savings goes up for ratepayers
- **Low-income carve-outs** within EERS are most exposed — relied most on IRA HEAR and WAP
- **States without EERS** have no legal floor — programs can simply be terminated when funding disappears

## Focus on Energy (Wisconsin) — Deep Dive

APTIM is the current Program Administrator for Focus on Energy — this is their flagship utility program.

- **Funder:** Wisconsin IOUs required by state statute to contribute 1.2% of annual retail revenue to Public Benefit Fund
- **Annual budget:** ~$95 million
- **Administrator:** APTIM Government Solutions, LLC (contracted through SEERA)
- **Scale:** 107 utility territories, 50,000+ projects/year, ~190,000 households served in 2024
- **ROI:** $5 in benefits for every $1 invested
- **Problem:** Demand is outpacing the $95M budget. A proposed bill (LRB-3626) would double utility contributions to 2.4% — not yet law
- **The gap:** IRA overlay programs (HOMES/HEAR) created demand that the base budget alone cannot sustain when federal funds freeze

### How It Works With Utilities
- Utilities contribute mandatory 1.2% of retail revenues → goes to SEERA → SEERA contracts APTIM to administer everything
- Utilities recover costs through rates
- Individual utilities can offer supplemental incentives beyond the base program (PSC approves case-by-case)
- IRA programs sit ON TOP of Focus on Energy as separate portfolios — separate budgets, compliance, reporting

### The Funding Gap Dynamic (Key Insight)
Higher demand = faster funding depletion = programs stall mid-year. Utilities have their own incentive buckets as a backstop, but utility incentives were designed to supplement, not replace state program funding. When IRA programs pause, customers who applied + contractors who geared up are left in limbo — APTIM must manage triage, renegotiate subcontractor scopes, and maintain regulatory compliance simultaneously.

## The Gap APTIM Can Fill (Service Opportunity)

When federal funding freezes mid-program, utilities face problems they cannot solve internally:

1. **Contractor network in limbo** — trained trade ally networks idle, risk of churn
2. **Customer pipeline management** — mid-project homeowners have expectation-based claims, regulatory complaints pile up
3. **Budget re-architecture** — rapidly re-modeling program economics requires TPA-level expertise utilities don't have in-house
4. **Compliance risk reallocation** — EERS targets still must be hit; TPA can model which alternative measures to scale up
5. **Reporting and grant closeout** — EM&V documentation must happen even mid-pause
6. **Rapid program redesign** — utilities are slow-moving; TPA provides operational capacity without 12-month procurement cycle

---

## Emerging Service Concept (Working Idea)

**Option A: Program Continuity Service**
APTIM formalizes a "program continuity" offering — a standing contract that utilities activate when federal/state funding freezes. APTIM manages contractor networks, customer pipelines, compliance documentation, and rapid program redesign during funding gaps.

**Option B: Alternative Funding Stacking**
APTIM helps utilities identify and access alternative funding sources beyond federal grants:
- Capacity market revenues (efficiency programs generate demand reduction value utilities can monetize)
- Carbon credit markets (energy savings monetized as offsets)
- On-bill financing (customers fund upgrades through utility bills — no grant needed)
- Green bonds (utilities issue ESG-rated bonds for efficiency programs)
- Utility CSR/foundation funds

**Option C: Small-Device Entry Program (Bridge Strategy)**
Low-cost small-device program (smart thermostats, LEDs, efficient appliances) that keeps utilities engaged and customer pipelines warm during funding gaps. Compounds into larger upgrades (heat pumps, insulation) when funding returns. Addresses EERS compliance at lower cost-per-customer.

**The bridge framing is strongest** — a low-cost program layer that keeps pipelines warm during funding gaps and converts into larger upgrades when money returns.
