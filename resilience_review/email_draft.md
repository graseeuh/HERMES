**3. Strategy Evaluation Analysis**
`resilience_review/evaluation_questions_analysis.md`

Applies the APTIM evaluation framework (May 22, 2026, Sections 2–3 and 6) to energy infrastructure strategies. Uses the APTIM two-step screen (identify regional system problem → compare strategy types) and the 1–5 scoring framework (operational value, implementation feasibility, long-term durability) across four strategy types: Protect, Accommodate, Avoidance/Relocation, and Regional Collaboration. Key finding: Regional Collaboration scores highest for long-term durability across all four installations (consistent with APTIM Section 3.2 conclusion). Watch conditions for utility systems (outage duration, restoration time, backup duration, SPOFs) are drawn directly from APTIM Section 6. Also notes where TAC Discussion Question 4 (utility resilience/redundancy discussions) connects to the APTIM framework.

**4. Energy Reliability Evaluation Methodology**
`resilience_review/evaluation_methodology.md`

Reproducible methodology using the MIRR's confirmed data sources: NEFRC CDBG flood models, NHC SLOSH + Resilient Jax for surge, NOAA/Resilient Jax 2022 SLR projections, Resilient Jax compound flood (2070), USDA Forest Service wildfire, North Florida MPO transportation network, Trust for Public Land heat severity, NWS Jacksonville wind, plus the energy-specific datasets from the prior intern dataset inventory (EIA Form 860, HIFLD, OpenEnergyHub, FEMA NFHL, USGS, NOAA SLR Calculator, natural gas pipelines). Includes a structured data gap table with five critical gaps confirmed through the MIRR TAC process and recommended next steps to close them.
