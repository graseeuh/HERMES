**Deliverables Included:**

**1. Adaptation Strategies Narrative (1-page)**
`resilience_review/adaptation_strategies_narrative.md`
Covers near-term (0–5 year) and long-term (5–20 year) strategies organized around the key risk themes: coastal substation exposure at NS Mayport, transmission corridor vulnerabilities serving NAS Jacksonville, and rural distribution reliability at Camp Blanding. Strategies include microgrid islanding, substation hardening, dedicated military transmission spurs, solar EUL development, and regional grid modernization advocacy.

**2. Energy Risk Map (Interactive)**
`resilience_review/energy_risk_map.html`
An interactive map (open in any browser) showing:
- Three installation locations with risk summaries
- Critical substations with risk ratings (Southside, Deer Park, Mayport Switching Station, Buckman, Orange Park)
- Transmission corridors with vulnerability ratings
- Flood/surge/wildfire risk zones (FEMA AE, NOAA Cat 3–4 surge, WUI)
- Adaptation priority sites with recommended actions and timelines
Layers are toggleable — all five layer groups can be turned on/off.

**3. Evaluation Questions Analysis**
`resilience_review/evaluation_questions_analysis.md`
Reviewed 8 standard resilience evaluation questions for applicability to energy infrastructure. Recommended modifications for all 8 questions, with the most significant changes targeting: non-climate risk inclusion (Q1), mission-impact consequence framing (Q2), DoD programmatic feasibility dimensions (Q3), bridging/near-term measures (Q5), quantitative monitoring metrics (Q7), and specific plan alignment references (Q8).

**4. Energy Reliability Evaluation Methodology**
`resilience_review/evaluation_methodology.md`
A full reproducible methodology covering: dataset inventory (EIA 860, HIFLD, FEMA NFHL, NOAA SLOSH, SLR viewer, USFS Wildfire Hazard Potential), 7 analytical steps (infrastructure topology → hazard overlay → vulnerability scoring → criticality weighting → risk scoring → adaptive capacity → scenario analysis), tools and workflow (QGIS / GeoPandas / NetworkX / Folium), and a structured gap analysis identifying critical gaps (on-installation asset data, substation condition), significant gaps (distributed solar inventory, SLOSH resolution), and methodological gaps (cascading failure modeling, demand-side uncertainty).
