# Energy Reliability Adaptation Strategies
## Northeast Military Installation Resilience Review
**Duval County, Clay County & Jacksonville, FL | Naval Air Station Jacksonville, Naval Station Mayport, Camp Blanding Joint Training Center**

---

### Current Energy Reliability Landscape

The three military installations anchoring the Northeast Florida defense corridor — NAS Jacksonville (Duval County), Naval Station Mayport (Duval County coastal), and Camp Blanding Joint Training Center (Clay County) — each depend on regional energy infrastructure that faces compounding climate-driven and operational stressors. NAS Jacksonville and NS Mayport draw from Jacksonville Electric Authority (JEA) and Florida Power & Light (FPL) transmission systems that traverse flood-prone corridors along the St. Johns River and Intracoastal Waterway. Camp Blanding, operating in a more rural Clay/Bradford County setting, relies on distribution infrastructure with limited redundancy across long rural line segments. All three installations sit within FEMA-designated Special Flood Hazard Areas or are bordered by them, placing substations, transformer vaults, and service laterals at recurring inundation risk.

Key vulnerabilities identified in prior geospatial analysis include: (1) coastal storm surge exposure at NS Mayport substations rated to Category 2 but proximate to Category 4 surge zones; (2) transmission line corridors shared between civilian and military loads with no dedicated military bypass; (3) aging distribution infrastructure (1950s–1970s vintage) along primary feeders serving NAS Jacksonville's flight operations and fuel storage areas; (4) wildland-urban interface fire risk to above-ground transmission serving Camp Blanding; and (5) systemic single-point-of-failure topology where loss of the Southside or Duval substations would simultaneously degrade service to multiple installations.

---

### Near-Term Adaptation Strategies (0–5 Years)

**Microgrids and On-Site Generation:** All three installations should accelerate the buildout of islanding-capable microgrids anchored by diesel or natural gas backup with solar-plus-storage overlay. NS Mayport's aircraft and submarine support operations represent mission-critical loads that justify dedicated microgrid investment using DoD ESTCP program funding. NAS Jacksonville's Integrated Logistics Complex and fueling infrastructure warrant similar priority. Near-term targets: 72-hour islanding capability for Tier 1 loads.

**Substation Hardening and Flood Barriers:** JEA's Deer Park and Southside substations, which supply primary feeders to NAS Jacksonville, should be assessed for deployable flood barriers (HESCO or permanent berm installations). Coordination with JEA under existing Cooperative Vulnerability Assessment agreements can accelerate permitting. For NS Mayport, above-ground transformer relocation or dry-type transformer installation eliminates the primary liquid-insulation spill risk during surge events.

**Mutual Aid and Interconnection Agreements:** Formalize Memoranda of Understanding with JEA and FPL for priority military restoration sequencing — equivalent to critical hospital status — within 90 days. Establish a joint energy coordination cell between NAVFAC Southeast and JEA's Emergency Operations to enable real-time grid status monitoring during named storm events.

---

### Long-Term Adaptation Strategies (5–20 Years)

**Dedicated Military Transmission Spurs:** The medium-to-long-term solution for NAS Jacksonville and NS Mayport is a dedicated 138 kV or 230 kV spur from a diverse grid injection point, decoupled from the civilian distribution backbone. This mirrors approaches taken at Eglin AFB (FL) and Norfolk Naval Station (VA). Capital cost is high ($40–120M range depending on routing), but eliminates the fundamental shared-infrastructure vulnerability.

**Climate-Resilient Substation Siting:** As the regional grid undergoes long-cycle asset replacement (2030–2040), advocate through Defense Installation Strategic Sustainability (DISS) planning for substation relocations to elevation-adequate sites (≥+15 ft NAVD88) and hardened enclosures rated for 175 mph wind loads consistent with projected Atlantic hurricane intensification trends.

**Renewable Energy Integration and Storage Scaling:** Camp Blanding's land availability (73,000+ acres) uniquely positions it for utility-scale solar development under Enhanced Use Lease (EUL) authorities, producing on-installation generation that reduces dependence on rural distribution. Combined with grid-scale battery storage (4–8 hour duration), this provides a climate-aligned, operationally resilient power supply for training operations and depot maintenance through 2050 and beyond.

**Regional Grid Modernization Advocacy:** Engage Florida Public Service Commission and FERC proceedings to ensure military installation interests are represented in the ongoing Northeast Florida transmission planning process. Climate-informed load forecasting (accounting for increased cooling demand and EV fleet electrification at installations) must be incorporated into IRP filings by JEA and FPL.
